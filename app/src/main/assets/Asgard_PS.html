Problem Statement

Asgard is a newly found planet just like Earth with the following alterations:

  1. Rotation period of 50 hours and revolution time of 489 days.
  2. Saline water covers 50 percent of the planet and fresh water is 2 percent of the entire water content.
  3. Existence of no lethal uncontrollable alien species.
  4. Transportation and communication technology have been developed to an extent that we can reach this planet in three months and any communication signal requires two days to get delivered.
  5. The average temperature of the planet is from three to ten degrees Celsius.The planet has no seasons.
  6. Presence of no fossil fuels.
  7. (Bonus point) Following point is not mandatory but extra points would be awarded if considered: Gravity is half of that of earth.

To cater to the needs of our ever increasing population and depleting resources, the human civilisation has to see it's dawn on Asgard, for a comfortable continuation of the species. You have been assigned this tough task to turn into an achievable probability. Describe how this could be done by designing a settlement for 10,000 people. Use your creativity while keeping in mind the above mentioned facts to build upon different aspects of a colony. For example production, distribution and storage of food, management of waste, the problems that would be faced, governing health of people.

How the Event would Proceed
  1. An abstract is to be submitted.This abstract should be at least 500-600 words.
  2. Your abstract would be evaluated on how many different aspects you have deliberated upon and how many of these are in line with the physics of the planet.
  3. On the basis of your abstract you would be selected for the second round.
  4. In the second round you need to present your abstract and ideas in the form of a presentation.
Format of the Project
  1. The project should be submitted in a pdf.
  2. There is no strict format that you need to stick to. Make it as comprehendible as possible.
Evaluation
  1. Your report would be evaluated on how many different aspects you have deliberated upon and how many of these are in line with the physics of the planet.
  2. The more descriptive you are, the more points your report will score.
